The **Chat** page lets you ask questions about your knowledge — your uploaded [Document Sources](/ai/document-sources) and your [trained cases](/ai/train-case) — and get answers in plain language. Every answer is drawn from your own documents and comes with citations, so you can see exactly where it came from.

Open **Chat** from the **AI** section of the left sidebar.

<Frame caption="Asking a question on the Chat page">
  ![Chat page](/doc-images/19.png)
</Frame>

## Pick what to chat with

At the top of the page, click the selector to choose which knowledge base to ask about. It opens with two tabs:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Documents" icon="file-lines">
    Your uploaded Document Sources — policies, manuals, agreements, and other files.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cases" icon="folder-open">
    Your trained cases. Each shows its case name and document count.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Use the search box at the top of the list to find a source quickly, then click it to select it. Your question only searches the source you've picked.

## Ask a question

Before you've asked anything, Xenora suggests a few starter questions about the selected source — click one to try it, or type your own.

Type into the **Ask anything…** box at the bottom and press Enter (or click the send arrow). The answer appears above, with citations linking back to the source documents. Keep asking follow-up questions in the same conversation — Xenora remembers the context of what you've already discussed.

To start fresh with a clean conversation, click **New chat** at the top of the page.

## Chat or Search

Below the input box you can switch between two modes:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Chat" icon="message">
    Get an **AI-written answer** to your question, in full sentences, with citations. Best for questions like *"What's the notice period in this policy?"*
  </Card>
  <Card title="Search" icon="magnifying-glass">
    Get back the **most relevant passages** from your documents, ranked — with no AI-written answer. Best when you want to find and read the exact wording yourself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Xenora picks a sensible default for you — lookup-style queries (like a section number or an exact phrase) lean towards **Search** — but you can switch modes any time before you send.

<Tip>
  Ask specific, focused questions for the best answers. *"What documents are still missing for this case?"* works better than *"Tell me about this case."* If an answer looks off, open the citation to check the source — and remember the AI can occasionally make mistakes, so verify anything important.
</Tip>
