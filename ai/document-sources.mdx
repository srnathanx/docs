A **Knowledge Base** is a private library of your own documents that Xenora's AI can read and answer questions about. Upload your policies, manuals, agreements, or reference material once — then ask questions about them in plain language from the [Chat](/ai/chat) page, and get answers backed by the exact source.

You build a knowledge base by creating a **Document Source**: you give it a name, add your files, and Xenora does the rest.

## Where to find it

Open **Document Sources** from the **AI** section of the left sidebar. This opens the **Knowledge Base** page, where all your sources live.

  ![Knowledge Base page](/doc-images/17.png)
</Frame>

The page has two tabs:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Document Source" icon="book">
    Knowledge bases you build from your own uploaded files — the focus of this page.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cases Source" icon="folder-open">
    Knowledge bases created automatically from your **trained cases**. See [Train a Case](/ai/train-case).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Each row in the list shows the source name, the number of documents in it, when it was created and last updated, and its current **Status**. A source is ready to chat with once its status shows **Ready**.

## Create a new Document Source

From the Knowledge Base page, click **Create New Document Source** in the top-right corner.

<Frame caption="Creating a new Document Source">
  ![Create Document Source form](/doc-images/18.png)
</Frame>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name your knowledge base">
    Enter a **Knowledge Base Name** — a short, descriptive title like "Support Knowledge Base" or "HR Policies". This is how you'll recognise it later in the chat selector.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a description">
    Write a one-line **Description** so your teammates understand what this knowledge base is for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your files">
    Under **Data Sources**, the **Files & Documents** option is selected by default. Drag and drop your files into the upload area, or click to browse and pick them from your computer.

    Supported file types: **PDF, DOC, DOCX, TXT, MD, XLS, XLSX, and JSONL**. You can add several files to the same knowledge base — each one appears in the list below the upload area with its size.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the source">
    Click **Create Document Source** at the bottom. Xenora reads and indexes your files so the AI can search them. This takes a few moments — the status starts as **Processing** and changes to **Ready** when it's done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Group related material into one knowledge base rather than mixing unrelated topics. A focused source ("HR Policies") gives sharper answers than a catch-all one, and it's easier to pick the right source when you start a chat.
</Tip>

## Managing a Document Source

Each source in the list has a set of actions on the right:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Re-train" icon="database">
    Refresh the knowledge base after you've added, removed, or updated files so the AI's answers reflect the latest content.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Edit" icon="pen">
    Change the name, description, or the files in the knowledge base.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Delete" icon="trash">
    Permanently remove the knowledge base and everything indexed from it. The AI can no longer answer questions about it. This can't be undone.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What's next

Once your source shows **Ready**, head to the [Chat](/ai/chat) page to start asking questions about it.
